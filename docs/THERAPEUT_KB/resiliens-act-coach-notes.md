## What ACT Coach is

Commercial ACT self-help app by Resiliens. Positions around **psychological flexibility**
and living according to values. Claims: 60K+ users, 200+ ACT exercises, 30+ guided
meditations, values clarification, progress tracking.

## The ACT Hexaflex (six core processes)

| Process | Dutch (Resiliens) | Core skill |
|---------|-------------------|------------|
| Present moment | Huidig moment | Full engagement with current experience |
| Acceptance | Acceptatie | Open up; make room for difficult feelings |
| Defusion | Defusie | Distance from thoughts; see them as thoughts |
| Self-as-context | Zelf-als-context | Contact with observing self |
| Values | Waarden | Clarify what truly matters |
| Committed action | Toegewijde actie | Effective action guided by values |

**Psychological flexibility** = ability to contact the present moment, accept internal
experience, defuse from unhelpful thoughts, connect with values, and take committed action.

## What ACT Coach does NOT emphasize (our differentiators)

1. **Psycho-education on avoidance cycle** — our Module 1 (pre-hexaflex foundation)
2. **Body work in every module** — psychosomatic integration throughout
3. **Three complaint types** — pain, mental, alcohol with conditional examples
4. **Crisis routing** — `/noodhulp`, 0800-0113, safety screening at onboarding
5. **Non-punitive relapse framing** — explicit in Module 7 and philosophy

## Technique categories (write own versions)

When filling module content, ensure each hexaflex process has **practical techniques**
in the therapist's voice. Categories inspired by ACT Coach (not copied):

### Acceptance (Module 2)
- Making room for sensation/emotion without fixing
- Willingness vs willfulness
- Breathing into discomfort (links to ademruimte body work)

### Defusion (Module 3)
- "I notice I'm having the thought that…"
- Thanking the mind / silly voice
- Observing inner critic as loudspeaker
- Body tension when fused with a thought (links to spanning & loslaten)

### Present moment (Module 4)
- Sensory anchors (5 senses)
- Single-task awareness
- Body as anchor in the now

### Self-as-context (Module 5)
- Observer vs content distinction
- "Who is noticing this?"
- Centering in the body

### Values (Module 6)
- Values vs goals distinction
- Life domains exploration (relationships, health, growth, etc.)
- "What would you do if pain were less dominant?" → direction, not cure

### Committed action (Module 7)
- Small values-aligned steps
- Flexible persistence
- Relapse as information about values

## Review questions (when comparing to ACT Coach)

When reviewing module content, ask:

1. Does this module teach the hexaflex process clearly?
2. Is there a body-based experiential component?
3. Are examples provided for pain, mental, and alcohol audiences?
4. Does copy avoid cure-promising language?
5. Is the tone warm and invitational (ACT-consistent)?
6. Is every sentence original (not Resiliens-derived)?
